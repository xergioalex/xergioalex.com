# From Idea to Y Combinator

### The DailyBot Story

<small>Sergio Alexander Florez · Startup Grind Pereira · 2026</small>

---

## 2017: The problem

Every morning, the same Slack ritual:

> "What did you do yesterday? What will you do today? Any blockers?"

We were 4 engineers. **The standup meeting was killing our flow.**

## The pivot moment

We weren't building a standup bot.

We were building an **async team collaboration platform**. <!-- .element: class="fragment fade-up" -->

Standups were just the entry point. <!-- .element: class="fragment fade-up" -->

---

## Growth: 0 to 10,000 teams

- **2017** — Internal tool for our team <!-- .element: class="fragment fade-up" -->
- **2018** — Public launch, first paying customers <!-- .element: class="fragment fade-up" -->
- **2019** — 1,000 teams, ramen profitable <!-- .element: class="fragment fade-up" -->
- **2020** — COVID remote work boom: 5,000+ teams <!-- .element: class="fragment fade-up" -->
- **2021** — Y Combinator S21 batch <!-- .element: class="fragment fade-up" -->

---

## Y Combinator S21

What we learned in 3 months:

1. **Talk to users every single day** — not once a quarter <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
2. **Do things that don't scale** — manual onboarding beats automated funnels early on <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
3. **Ship weekly** — if you're not embarrassed by your last release, you shipped too late <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->

---

## The hard parts nobody talks about

- **Year 2 dip** — initial excitement fades, growth slows, you question everything <!-- .element: class="fragment fade-up" -->
- **Cofounder dynamics** — alignment is a daily practice, not a one-time conversation <!-- .element: class="fragment fade-up" -->
- **Saying no** — the features you don't build define your product as much as the ones you do <!-- .element: class="fragment fade-up" -->

---

## What I'd tell my 2017 self

1. **Start charging from day one.** Free users give feedback. Paying users give *useful* feedback. <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
2. **Build for the workflow, not the feature.** Nobody wants a bot — they want their mornings back. <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
3. **Distribution is the product.** Being inside Slack/Teams isn't a feature — it's the entire strategy. <!-- .element: class="fragment" -->
